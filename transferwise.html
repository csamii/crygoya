What is Transfer Wise

TransferWise was launched in 2011 by two good friends, Taavet and Kristo with the vision of making international money transfers cheap, fair, and simple. They’re both from Estonia. Taavet was the first employee at Skype, Kristo worked for Deloitte and lived in London.

In the past 10 years, the TransferWise movement has spread far and wide. Trusted by Over 10 million happy customers moving more than £4.5 billion every month, saving them £3 million in bank fees every day.

Why People Trust TRANSFER WISE

Richard Branson, and PayPal co-founder Max Levchin, has an investment with TransferWise.

TransferWise has opened 14 offices, with over 2,200 employees.

TransferWise have added the multi-currency account, which lets you hold over 50 currencies at once and convert them whenever they like.

You can also get a TransferWise debit Mastercard to spend in over 150 currencies too.

TRANSFER WISE’s service stands out for 5 key features:

Fees up to 90% lower than banks;

Quick transfer that allows you to receive the money within 24 hours or less on average;

Conveniently apply for transfer in 5 minutes, anywhere anytime using its mobile app or website;

Transparent transfer process that gives both your and the recipient real-time notifications on the transfer status;

Perfectly secure blockchain-based platform with all your sensitive information encrypted.

User Convenience

To optimize user experience, TRANSFERWISE provides you with:

Real-time comparison of fee with commercial banks

Calculation of the exact amount of receiving amount before you send money

Remittance information saving function so that you don’t have to waste your time entering recipient details every time you send to the same person

TRANSFERWISE Fees and Exchange Rate

TransferWise’s exchange rates are competitive and are typically between 0.5% and 2% worse than mid-market rates. Create an account with TransferWise and log in to see the exchange rates you will be charged, depending on where you are sending to. Exchange rates may vary based on several factors:

How much money you are transferring.

Where you are transferring money to.

How you are funding your money transfer.

The transfer service you choose.
